Guidance for Italics

Use to:

  • Denote scientific terms
  • Publication titles

Avoid:

  • Use in links, Headings, or Titles

Options:

Guidance for Red Text

Use to:

  • Emphasize critical or time-sensitive content (i.e. outbreaks, vaccination guidance, critical document changes)
  • Indicate error messages
  • Denote required fields or field errors in a form
  • Alert users to outages or global system issues

Avoid:

  • Placement on a background color other than white

Do not:

  • Use for links, Headings, or Titles

Try instead:

  • Use Containers and their flexible style options to emphasize new information, dates, page changes, or document changes

Blockquotes

Blockquotes are used to emphasize and reference quotes from an individual in an article or another source. They typically have a unique style as a way to highlight a specific point or face in an article and are displayed withing paragraphs of a content page.

Blockquote Examples

XS SM MD LG XL XXL

Blockquote Specifications

  • Quote Text: Open Sans, Block Quote, black
  • Source Text: always preceded by a hyphen, Open Sans, Small Text, gray_darker
  • Padding: p-3 (top, right, bottom, left)
  • Margin: m-3 (bottom only)
  • Borders:
    • Accent: br-1, bl-3, b-gray-l3
    • Full: bt-1, br-1, bb-1, bl-3, b-gray-l3 for all
  • Options
    • Different background and border styles can be achieved using Containers
    • Quotes may be added automatically to the Quote Text using the .with-quote class on a blockquote element, or an element styled with .blockquote

Footnote Indicators

Footnote Indicators are used to connect specific information to references or supplemental information that are usually displayed toward the bottom of the content. They can be set as links to allow users to jump down to the references or unlinked.
